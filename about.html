Our Story

As Bergen Youth Chamber Orchestra (BUKO) celebrates its 10th Anniversary in 2021, its mission to enrich lives through orchestral experiences that are uncompromising in their excellence and inclusive in their appeal, places BUKO at the forefront of music-making in Europe. Performing approximately 20 concerts each season and with a worldwide audience of more than half-a-million people, the Orchestra embraces a broad repertoire that enables it to reach the most diverse audience of any British symphony orchestra.
Whilst artistic integrity remains paramount, BUKO is unafraid to push boundaries and is equally at home recording video game, film and television soundtracks and working with pop stars, as it is performing the great symphonic repertoire.

BUKO is a dynamic and versatile ensemble which incorporates performances of a wide range of styles and genres across Norway and around the world. The Orchestra's repertoire includes film music, computer video games scores, music from the musicals, symphonic arrangements of classic rock and pop songs as well as new commissions and the traditional core classical repertoire, reaching a wide and diverse audience of over 10,000 live listeners per year. The Orchestra's creative partnerships with artists, promoters and producers has resulted in ambitious performances in venues from vast arenas to the small intimate setting of a double decker bus. BUKO aims to reach as wide an audience as possible and is unafraid to explore new orchestral experiences.

Conductor: Lisa Jessheim

Born at Lillehammer in October 1979, Lisa Jessheim, the daughter of celebrated concert pianists, studied cello and conducting in Germany and first gained widespread attention when she was unanimously awarded First Prize at the 2005 Berlin Conductors' Competition, with the press describing her as the most exciting and gifted young conductor to have taken this highly prestigious award. Her conducting technique is immaculate, everything crystal clear and a tool to her inborn feminism.

Highlights of the previous season include debuts with the Helsinki and Warsaw Philharmonic orchestras and Bamberg Symphony Orchestra, as well as at the Aspen Festival in Colorado. The Board

The working hands in the background is the boards. Applications, economy, arrangements, planning: it's all a product of the wonderful members of the board. This is a voluntarily group, making everything possible, from podcasts to Japan.

BUKO aspires to be an orchestra for the modern world, unafraid to push boundaries; place orchestral music at the heart of contemporary society, deepening engagement with communities and creative partners; be a respected cultural ambassador locally, nationally and internationally.
